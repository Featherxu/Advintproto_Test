THE BRIEF

Prototyping with physical controllers. Using processing to connect the interaction between graphic and different controllers and sensors.

INSPIRATION

I got the inspiration when i was tidying up my documents and saw my Artcenter offer by chance.

I love patterns, and these dots reminds me the artwork from Yayoi Kusama and her bold color choice. I think it's interesting to explore it in processing and the controllers.  if users could play with it, it would be very fascinating. That's how I got my inspiration and decide to work on this topic.

ENVISION
CONTROLLER

HOW VISION CHANGED

My patterns have two elements: the color and the size of dots. At the beginning i was exploring the size of it using the potentiometer, it was fun but I realized the patterns only looks best when the size of the whole dot is large enough. Moreoever, according to my inspiration from Yayoi Kusama, I decided to focus more on the color change. That's also the reason I choose the photocell as the sensor in stead of a button later.

POTENTIOMETER

The potentiometer controls the color of the dots by HSB color system. By turning it, the color will change gradually from red to blue, and the brightness and saturation all stays the same.

PHOTOCELL

It detects the amount of light that can get to the censor. If it's very bright, the background color will be white, while when no light comes in at all, the color will be dark. users can use their finger to move closer to the sensor and see how the brightness on the graphic would change.

PHYSICAL
CONTROLLER

HOW I SETTLED ON IT

I used  cardboards to make a box for my controllers. There's a lot of wires and components and I don't want the users getting confused while interacting with it. As a result I covered the materials and only shows the ones they can interact with, which is a potentiometer and the photocell.
‍
I noticed that it's hard to use fingers to fully covered the photocell and fingers can easily break it. So I make a little "window" for users to interact with it, by slide the window open and off.
